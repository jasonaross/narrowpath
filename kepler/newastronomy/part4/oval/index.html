Advice to readers on chapters 46-50

Don't read these chapters! Or, at least don't linger on them. They are incredibly difficult, and can take as much time to work through as the entire remainder of Part IV.

f

It is not without reason that Kepler writes in chapter 45 that “But then what they say in the proverb, ‘a hasty dog bears blind pups’, happened to me.” The chapters in which Kepler implements his oval hypothesis are very difficult, and, taken together, can take almost as much mental energy as the remainder of Part IV. For this reason, it is not advisable to work thoroughly through all the chapters in this section the first time through the book, or, if you do, remember what happens in the preceding four chapters 41-44. Kepler has proven that Mars cannot move in a circle, and that the orbit must be brought in at the sides.
