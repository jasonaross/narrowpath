Chapter 26

This is by no means a complete conception of chapter 26, but rather a simple animation to look at the effect of changing the length of αη -- the distance of the Earth from the Sun. In the following animation, the s key increases, and the x key decreases, this distance. Point η is the lower right earth position. By changing its distance only slightly (which entails a very small change in the geocentric position of Mars), the line of apsides and eccentricity change rather dramatically. The other two positions of the Earth, ζ and δ, stay at the same place. Since three points make a circle, changing the position of η changes the size and center of the circle.

Kepler suspects the length of αη that he determined to be in error. When he uses all three points to make a circle, he gets a line of apsides that is almost 10° away from where he expected it to be. By making a small change to αη, the apsides come to be more in line with what he expected, and the eccentricity becomes close to the value of 1800 for a bisected eccentricity. Since planetary position η is so close to the line from Mars to the sun, a small change in the geocentric observed position of Mars has a proportionally large impact on the distance.
